Which two websites are you most inspired by?

A List Apart and Stet: A Writers' Journal on Culture & Technology

How do they exhibit effective use of space, size or balance?

A List Apart

The large top image is often a graytone illustration, while the site and article titels are thick, black sans-serifs that frame the drawings without visual competition.

However, in cases where the main top image is either a screencap or a photograph, and in articles where in-article illustrations contain text, there is a fair amount of visual conflict, either due to weight of images or to the fact that they are "off-stylesheet."

Stet: A Writers' Journal on Culture & Technology

Large, full-color top image evokes print magazine masthead and occupies the ENTIRE screen "above the fold." The artile and site titles are proportional to one another—the same font size, but different font styles. The above-the-fold image typically contains a ton of negative space.

Pulling author bio to side, using a mid grey rather than article-black sets up nice hierarchy. Ditto for editor's notes, pull-quoted in left margin.

What makes them similar and different?

similar

The size and spacing create an etremely clear visual hierachy. The column width—and thick, empty white margins to left and right of article text—are effective, as is the vertical spacing between paragraphs. these all contribute to high readability.

There is never/rarely competition between content. Size and spacing create a clear visual hierarchy. Both sites would probably function very well across screen sizes.

different

The size-variation in A List Apart feels too-much. Body text is slightly too small to read comfortably.

Articles in Stet feel more stand-alone, like they're independent sites. A List apart feels more like it's part of a large body of work, and has more emphasis on navigating between articles.
